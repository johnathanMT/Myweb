The Hidden Traditions

Occultism encompasses philosophical, religious, and magical traditions that seek knowledge of hidden truths beyond ordinary human understanding. Far from superstition, the great occult traditions were the intellectual heritage of their time — Pythagoras, Giordano Bruno, Isaac Newton, and Carl Jung all engaged deeply with occult philosophy.

Key Traditions

Alchemy

The proto-science of transmutation — seeking to transform base metals into gold and the soul into enlightenment.

Kabbalah

Jewish mystical tradition exploring the Tree of Life, the Sefirot, and the nature of the Divine.

Hermeticism

Based on the Corpus Hermeticum — "As above, so below." A philosophy of cosmic correspondence.

Rosicrucianism

17th century secret fraternity claiming ancient wisdom, combining Christianity, Kabbalah, and alchemy.

Ceremonial Magic

Ritual work involving planetary seals, invocations, and the Goetia — systematised occult practice.

Theosophy

H.P. Blavatsky's synthesis of Eastern and Western mysticism — karma, reincarnation, cosmic evolution.
